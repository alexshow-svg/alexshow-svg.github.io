The simple principle

Decide how much of your account you are willing to lose on a trade, then size the position so that the stop-loss defines that loss.

This is usually expressed as a percentage of the account (for example: 0.25%, 0.5%, or 1% per trade).

Smaller risk does not mean smaller profits. It means smoother equity curves and longer survival.
