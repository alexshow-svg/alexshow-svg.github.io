Risk per trade: the 1% rule (and when it fails)

Educational only. No predictions. Just survival logic.

What it is

“Risk 1% per trade” means: if your stop-loss is hit, you lose about 1% of your account. Not 1% position size — 1% loss.

Why it matters

Risk per trade controls how fast you can blow up. Small risk keeps you in the game long enough to learn.

Common mistakes

  • Confusing “1% position” with “1% risk”.
  • Moving stops wider after entry (“hope management”).
  • Taking multiple correlated trades that add up to more than 1% total risk.

When 1% can still be too much

  • If you take many trades per day (risk stacks).
  • If your strategy has long losing streaks.
  • If you trade highly volatile assets with slippage.

Simple rule

Start smaller than you think (0.25%–1%), then increase only after you prove consistency.
