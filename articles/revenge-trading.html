Revenge trading: why it happens and how to stop

Educational only. Built for real humans with real emotions.

What it is

Revenge trading is when a loss triggers a need to “get it back” fast — causing bigger size, worse entries, and broken rules.

Why it happens

  • Loss feels like an attack on your identity (“I was wrong”).
  • Urgency narrows your thinking.
  • Your brain wants emotional relief, not good decisions.

The warning signs

  • Increasing size after a loss
  • Taking trades you normally ignore
  • Refreshing charts constantly
  • Anger, shame, “I must fix this now”

A simple stop plan

  1. After any big emotional hit: pause 10 minutes (no charts).
  2. Write 2 sentences: “What rule did I follow?” and “What rule did I break?”
  3. Set a daily max loss (e.g. 2R). If hit, you’re done for the day.

If you want one rule: don’t trade to feel better. Trade only when your plan says so.
